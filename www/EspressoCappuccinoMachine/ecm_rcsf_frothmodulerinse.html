    FROTH MODULE RINSE
    This operation can be completed at any time to ensure best performance.
    Cleanliness of the Froth Module is important to prevent discharging spoiled milk residue into a drink and to ensure good quality frothed milk. Poor quality frothed milk may be an indication that the Froth Module needs to be rinsed. If the results of the rinsing process are unsatisfactory, the Froth Module should be removed, disassembled, and thoroughly washed per the instructions in Section 5F.
    OBSERVE the ON/OFF light ring is Green, indicating the unit is in the STANDBY ON mode, and all indicators are steady ON.
    FROTH MODULE
    1. EMPTY all milk from the Froth Module and rinse all surfaces of the Froth Module with clean water. If needed, wash the Froth Module surfaces with soap and water, then rinse.
    2. FILL the Froth Module with clean fresh water* to the “MAX” level shown on the Froth Module Reservoir.
    RECEIVING CUP
    PLACE an empty cup in the LEFT receiving cup space.
    OBSERVE the CLEAN button will be lit. PRESS and HOLD the CLEAN button until the Espresso/Cappuccino Machine pumps turn on (approximately three seconds).
    RECEIVING CUP
    EMPTY the receiving cup. Any water collected should be disposed of in the galley sink.

    NOTE: The platen drip tray is not intended for disposal of large volumes of liquid. Do not pour liquids into the drip tray.
    * Instead of water to rinse the Froth Module, a cleaning solution may be used to clean the Froth Module. The cleaning solution can include any of the following:
    1. 500mL cold water with 5mL antibacterial dishwashing liquid.
    2. 500mL cold water with 15mL Full Circle® milk cleaning product.
    3. 500mL cold water with 30mL Rinza® milk cleaning product.
    4. Other milk cleaning solutions per cleaning product manufacturer instructions.
